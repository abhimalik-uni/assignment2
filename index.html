Melbourne Real Estate Analysis

FIT3179 Visualisation 2 - Abhi Malik

Real estate in Victoria, Australia, is a dynamic market characterized by a diverse range of properties, from urban apartments to sprawling suburban homes and picturesque rural estates. Victoria, home to the vibrant city of Melbourne, often showcases a blend of modern architecture and historic charm. The demand for housing is influenced by factors such as population growth, economic development, and lifestyle preferences. Additionally, the state's government plays a role through housing policies and initiatives to promote sustainable and affordable living options.

The average number of properties sold per suburb across 2016 and 2017 was 43 properties. The sales score represents a standardised score ranging from -1 to 1 which is scaled based on a ratio of the number of properties sold and the total number of properties in that suburb. The average sales score is represented by 0 whilst suburbs that performed below this average are negative and positive for above the average. Almost all suburbs with a positive sales score were within a 15.5 kilometre radius from the city centre. The hottest suburbs with sales scores of 1 were the neighbouring suburbs of Essendon West and Niddrie in Melbourne's North West.

The median purchase price across all properties was 903,000 AUD. Kooyong boasted the highest suburb median price of 2,185,000 AUD. There is a strong correlation between the median prices of the suburbs and its location relative to the Yarra river, Central Business District and the coast. Most of the suburbs with the highest median prices were located below the Yarra river, closer to the CBD and also along the shoreline.

The majority of the properties were sold in the winter season (June, July and August) and the least were sold in the summer season (December, January and February). However, the number of sales were inversely proportional to the median price of the properties sold in that season. The summer season had a median property price of 957,500 AUD, almost 11% greater than winter's median price.
